PERMUTATION & COMBINATION

The single most important question: Is ORDER important? YES → Permutation. NO → Combination. That one question eliminates 70% of confusion. Master the two formulas and the circular rule.

🔍The Decision Tree — Most Important
📐All Formulas
Permutation — Order Matters
nPr = n! / (n−r)!
Arranging r items from n. Each different arrangement is unique.
🔑 Keywords: arrange, seating, password, rank, positions
Combination — Order Doesn't Matter
nCr = n! / [r!(n−r)!]
Selecting r items from n. AB and BA are the SAME selection.
🔑 Keywords: select, choose, committee, team, group
Circular Arrangement
(n−1)!
Arrange n people around a circular table. One position is fixed.
🔑 5 people at round table = 4! = 24 ways
Factorial Values — Memorize!
0!=1, 1!=1, 2!=2, 3!=6, 4!=24, 5!=120
6!=720, 7!=5040. These come up constantly — don't calculate, memorize!
⚠️ 0! = 1 (NOT 0 — very common mistake!)
Repeated Letters
n! / (p! × q! × ...)
Divide by factorial of each repeated letter count.
🔑 AAB: 3!/2! = 3 | MISSISSIPPI: 11!/(4!×4!×2!)
Key Relationship
nPr = nCr × r!
Permutation = Combination × Ways to arrange. Selection × Arrangement.
💡 Helps when switching between P and C questions

🧠Interactive Q&A
QUESTION 1 — EASY
In how many ways can 3 students be selected from a class of 7?
✅ B) 35

"Selected" → ORDER DOESN'T MATTER → COMBINATION
7C3 = 7! / (3! × 4!) = (7×6×5) / (3×2×1) = 210/6 = 35
QUESTION 2 — MODERATE
In how many ways can 4 people be seated in a row of 4 chairs?
✅ C) 24

"Seated in a row" → ORDER MATTERS → PERMUTATION
4P4 = 4! = 4×3×2×1 = 24
QUESTION 3 — EXAM MODERATE
How many ways can 5 people sit around a circular table?
✅ B) 24

Circular arrangement formula: (n−1)!
= (5−1)! = 4! = 4×3×2×1 = 24
Note: In circular, one person's position is fixed, others arrange around them.

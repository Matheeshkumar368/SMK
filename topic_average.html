📐All Formulas
Formula 1 — Basic Average
Average = Sum / Count
The golden formula. Sum = Average × Count. Count = Sum ÷ Average.
🔑 If avg marks = 75, students = 8 → Total marks = 600
Formula 2 — New Average (Member Joins)
New Sum = Old Sum + New Value
When a new person joins, add their value and recalculate average with n+1.
🔑 New Avg = New Sum / (n+1)
Formula 3 — New Average (Member Leaves)
New Sum = Old Sum − Leaving Value
When a person leaves, subtract their value and recalculate with n-1.
🔑 New Avg = New Sum / (n-1)
Formula 4 — Replacement Effect
Change in Sum = (New − Old) value
If one member is replaced, change in avg = (New−Old)/n
🔑 If avg rises by 2 for 5 people → new person is 2×5=10 more than old
Formula 5 — Average of Consecutive Numbers
Avg = (First + Last) / 2
For consecutive numbers, the average is simply the middle value.
🔑 Avg of 1 to 99 = (1+99)/2 = 50
Formula 6 — Weighted Average
Avg = (n₁x₁ + n₂x₂) / (n₁+n₂)
When two groups with different averages are combined.
🔑 Used when "two classes merge" type questions appear

⚡Shortcut Tricks
🔥 Replacement Shortcut — Never Re-Calculate!
If average of n people changes by d after replacement:
New person's value = Old person's value + n × d
Example: 10 people, avg increases by 3 after replacing someone aged 25
→ New person's age = 25 + 10×3 = 55
🔥 Consecutive Numbers Average
Avg of 1 to n = (n+1)/2
Avg of 1 to 100 = 101/2 = 50.5
Avg of first n odd numbers = n
Avg of first n even numbers = n+1
❌ Common Trap Questions
TRAP: "Average speed" ≠ (speed1 + speed2) / 2 ❌
Use: 2ab/(a+b) formula for average speed

TRAP: If average of 4 numbers is 20, and a 5th number 10 is added,
New avg = (4×20 + 10)/5 = 90/5 = 18 (not 15 or 20)

🧠Interactive Q&A
QUESTION 1 — EASY
The average of 5 numbers is 40. If one number 30 is removed, what is the new average?
✅ B) 42.5

Old sum = 5 × 40 = 200
New sum = 200 − 30 = 170
New avg = 170 / 4 = 42.5
QUESTION 2 — MODERATE
Average age of 10 students is 15. A new student joins and the average becomes 15.5. What is the age of the new student?
✅ C) 20.5

Old sum = 10 × 15 = 150
New sum = 11 × 15.5 = 170.5
New student's age = 170.5 − 150 = 20.5
QUESTION 3 — EXAM MODERATE
In a group of 8 people, average age is 30. If one person aged 20 is replaced by someone, and the new average becomes 32, what is the age of the new person?
✅ C) 36

Shortcut: New person = Old person + n × change in avg
= 20 + 8 × (32−30) = 20 + 8×2 = 20 + 16 = 36

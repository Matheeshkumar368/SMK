PROFIT & LOSS

Everything is based on Cost Price (CP). If you sell above CP → Profit. Below CP → Loss. Discount is always on Marked Price. Profit% is always on CP. Remember these two rules and half the topic is done.

📐All Formulas — Complete Arsenal
Formula 1 — Profit
Profit = SP − CP
Selling Price minus Cost Price. Always positive when profit.
🔑 If SP > CP → Profit situation
Formula 2 — Loss
Loss = CP − SP
Cost Price minus Selling Price. Always positive when loss.
🔑 If CP > SP → Loss situation
Formula 3 — Profit Percentage
Profit% = (Profit/CP) × 100
ALWAYS on CP. Never on SP. This is the most common mistake.
⚠️ Profit% is ALWAYS calculated on CP
Formula 4 — Loss Percentage
Loss% = (Loss/CP) × 100
Same rule — always on CP. CP = Base for all calculations.
⚠️ Loss% is ALWAYS calculated on CP
Formula 5 — SP from Profit%
SP = CP × (100 + P%) / 100
Find selling price when profit% and CP are given.
🔑 CP=500, P%=20 → SP = 500×120/100 = 600
Formula 6 — SP from Loss%
SP = CP × (100 − L%) / 100
Find selling price when loss% and CP are given.
🔑 CP=500, L%=10 → SP = 500×90/100 = 450
Formula 7 — Marked Price & Discount
SP = MP × (100 − D%) / 100
Discount is ALWAYS on Marked Price (MP). SP = MP after discount.
⚠️ Discount is on MP, Profit is on CP
Formula 8 — False Weight Profit
Profit% = (True−False)/False × 100
When shopkeeper uses false weights, calculate actual profit.
🔑 Claims 1000g but gives 900g → Profit = 100/900 × 100 = 11.1%

⚡Shortcut Tricks
🔥 The Two Golden Rules of P&L
Rule 1: Profit% and Loss% are ALWAYS on CP
Rule 2: Discount% is ALWAYS on MP (Marked Price)

Companies set traps by asking "30% discount on MP and 20% profit on SP" — don't fall for it!
🔥 Multiplier Method — Super Fast!
20% profit → SP = CP × 1.20
15% loss → SP = CP × 0.85
30% discount on MP → SP = MP × 0.70
Just multiply by (1 ± rate/100) directly!
❌ Hexaware's Favourite Traps
TRAP 1: "Shopkeeper gains 20% profit" → They ask Loss% sometimes using SP as base ❌
Always use CP as base for Profit/Loss%.

TRAP 2: "Sold at 20% profit, then 20% loss" → Net result is NOT 0%
Net = a+b+ab/100 = 20+(−20)+(20×−20/100) = −4% loss

🧠Interactive Q&A
QUESTION 1 — EASY
A shopkeeper buys an article for ₹400 and sells it for ₹500. What is the profit percentage?
✅ B) 25%

Profit = SP − CP = 500 − 400 = ₹100
Profit% = (100/400) × 100 = 25%
Remember: Profit% on CP, not SP!
QUESTION 2 — MODERATE
An item is marked at ₹800 and sold at 20% discount. If CP is ₹500, what is the profit%?
✅ A) 28%

SP = MP × (100−D%)/100 = 800 × 80/100 = ₹640
Profit = 640 − 500 = ₹140
Profit% = (140/500) × 100 = 28%
QUESTION 3 — EXAM MODERATE
A trader sells at 20% profit, then sells at 20% loss. What is the net result?
✅ B) 4% loss

Net = a + b + ab/100 where a=+20, b=−20
= 20 + (−20) + (20×−20)/100
= 0 − 400/100 = −4% (loss)
⚠️ This is Hexaware's #1 P&L trap!

📐The ONE Formula You Need

THE GOLDEN RULE: Ratio = (Higher Value − Mean Value) : (Mean Value − Lower Value)

Cross Subtraction Method
(H − M) : (M − L)
H = Higher value, M = Mean (desired) value, L = Lower value. Always: Higher−Mean gives LOWER ratio. Mean−Lower gives HIGHER ratio.
🔑 The most used formula in placement exams
Water Addition Formula
(New%−Old%) / (100−New%) × Quantity
When water is added to reduce concentration of milk or mixture.
🔑 Hexaware's favourite water addition question
Mixture Replacement
Final = Initial × (1 − x/V)^n
When x litres is removed and replaced with water, repeated n times.
🔑 V = total volume, x = replaced amount, n = times
📊Visual: Cross Subtraction Diagram
📌 Read the Diagram: Tea worth ₹20/kg mixed with ₹50/kg to get ₹30/kg mixture
Higher(50) − Mean(30) = 20 → This is the ratio for the LOWER quantity
Mean(30) − Lower(20) = 10 → This is the ratio for the HIGHER quantity
Ratio of (₹20 tea) : (₹50 tea) = 20 : 10 = 2 : 1
⚡Shortcuts & Tricks
🔥 The Cross Subtraction Rule — Memorize Forever
Step 1: Draw Mean value in the center.
Step 2: Write Lower on left, Higher on right.
Step 3: Cross-subtract: Higher − Mean gives left (lower) ratio.
Step 4: Mean − Lower gives right (higher) ratio.
That's it. No equations needed!
❌ Never Use Equations in Alligation!
WRONG WAY: 20x + 50y = 30(x+y) → solve for x and y (takes 2 minutes) ❌
RIGHT WAY: Cross Subtraction → (50−30):(30−20) = 20:10 = 2:1 ✅ (15 seconds)
❌ Most Common Mistake — Wrong Subtraction Order
Students sometimes write: Lower − Mean or Mean − Higher
ALWAYS: Higher − Mean = lower side ratio | Mean − Lower = higher side ratio

🧠Interactive Q&A
QUESTION 1 — EASY
Milk costing ₹20/litre is mixed with milk costing ₹50/litre to get a mixture at ₹30/litre. What is the ratio?
✅ A) 2:1

Using Cross Subtraction:
Higher(50) − Mean(30) = 20 (ratio for ₹20 milk)
Mean(30) − Lower(20) = 10 (ratio for ₹50 milk)
Ratio of ₹20 milk : ₹50 milk = 20:10 = 2:1
QUESTION 2 — MODERATE
Tea worth ₹40/kg and ₹60/kg are mixed to get tea worth ₹48/kg. In what ratio are they mixed?
✅ C) 3:2

Higher(60) − Mean(48) = 12 (ratio for ₹40 tea)
Mean(48) − Lower(40) = 8 (ratio for ₹60 tea)
Ratio ₹40 : ₹60 = 12:8 = 3:2
QUESTION 3 — EXAM MODERATE
20 litres of mixture contains 20% water. How much water should be added to make it 40% water?
✅ C) 6.67L

Water to add = (New% − Old%) / (100 − New%) × Quantity
= (40 − 20) / (100 − 40) × 20
= 20/60 × 20 = 6.67 litres

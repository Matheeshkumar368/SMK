⚡Shortcut Tricks
🔥 Area Doubles/Halves Relationships
Side doubles → Square area = 4x (quadruples!)
Radius doubles → Circle area = 4x (quadruples!)
Side halves → Square area = ¼ of original
Area ∝ (side)² — always square the ratio for areas!
🔥 π = 22/7 Shortcuts
Circumference of circle with r=7 → 2×22/7×7 = 44 (no calculator needed!)
Area of circle with r=7 → 22/7×49 = 154
Always use r=7 or r=14 examples to avoid messy decimals.
❌ Common Mistakes
WRONG: Side doubles → area doubles ❌
RIGHT: Side doubles → area quadruples (2² = 4) ✅

WRONG: Perimeter of circle = πr² ❌
RIGHT: Circumference = 2πr, Area = πr² ✅

🧠Interactive Q&A
QUESTION 1 — EASY
Find the area of a circle with radius 7 cm. (π = 22/7)
✅ C) 154 cm²

Area = πr² = (22/7) × 7 × 7 = 22 × 7 = 154 cm²
QUESTION 2 — MODERATE
The side of a square is doubled. By what factor does the area increase?
✅ C) 4 times

Original area = a² | New side = 2a | New area = (2a)² = 4a²
Area increased by factor 4
Rule: Area ∝ side², so if side×2, area×4
QUESTION 3 — EXAM MODERATE
Volume of a cylinder with radius 7cm and height 10cm. (π = 22/7)
✅ B) 1540 cm³

V = πr²h = (22/7) × 7² × 10 = (22/7) × 49 × 10 = 22 × 7 × 10 = 1540 cm³

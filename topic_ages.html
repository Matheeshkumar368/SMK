🔍The 3 Golden Rules — Memorize Forever
📐All Formulas
Formula 1 — X Years Ago
Age at that time = Present − X
10 years ago → subtract 10 from current age.
🔑 Present age 25, 5 years ago = 25−5 = 20
Formula 2 — X Years Hence
Future age = Present + X
10 years hence/later → add 10 to current age.
🔑 Present age 30, after 10 years = 30+10 = 40
Formula 3 — Difference Rule
Age difference is CONSTANT
If father is 30 years older than son, this stays 30 years forever.
🔑 Hexaware's favourite trap — "After 20 years, difference?" Same!
Formula 4 — Ratio Questions
Part = ratio/(sum) × Total
When ages are in ratio x:y, use ratio formula to find individual ages.
🔑 Father:Son = 5:2, total 56 → Father = 5/7 × 56 = 40
Formula 5 — Average Age
Total age = Avg × Count
Find total age from average, then use individual differences.
🔑 Average age 20, 5 people → Total = 100 years
Formula 6 — n times as old
A = n × B (present) | Solve for X years
Set up present age equation, then apply +/- for past/future conditions.
🔑 "Father is 3 times son's age" → F = 3S
⚡Smart Tricks
🔥 Difference Never Changes — 3 Second Solver
Q: Father's age is 40, son's age is 10. What will be the difference after 20 years?
WRONG WAY: Calculate 40+20=60, 10+20=30, difference=30...
RIGHT WAY: Difference NEVER changes = 40−10 = 30
Answer in 3 seconds without any calculation!
🔥 Smart Option Elimination
For "age X years hence" questions, the answer MUST be greater than current age.
For "age X years ago" questions, the answer MUST be less than current age.
Eliminate options that violate common sense immediately — saves 30 seconds!
❌ Never Confuse Ago and Hence
WRONG: "10 years ago" → ADD 10 to present age ❌
RIGHT: "10 years ago" → SUBTRACT 10 ✅

WRONG: Father is 30 years older. After 20 years, difference = 50 years ❌
RIGHT: Difference NEVER changes = 30 years ✅

🧠Interactive Q&A
QUESTION 1 — EASY
The present age of A is 36 years. 6 years hence, what will be A's age?
✅ C) 42

"Hence" → ADD
36 + 6 = 42 years
QUESTION 2 — MODERATE
Father's age is 4 times son's age. After 20 years, father will be twice son's age. What is son's current age?
✅ C) 10

Let son's age = x, Father = 4x
After 20 years: 4x+20 = 2(x+20)
4x+20 = 2x+40 → 2x = 20 → x = 10
QUESTION 3 — EXAM MODERATE
Hexaware Trap: A is 20 years older than B. After 10 years, what will be the difference between their ages?
✅ C) 20 years

Age DIFFERENCE never changes!
A is currently 20 years older than B → This remains 20 years after any number of years.
⚠️ This is the most popular trap question from this topic!

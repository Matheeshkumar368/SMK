PROBABILITY Mastery

Probability = Favourable outcomes ÷ Total outcomes. Always between 0 and 1. Impossible = 0. Certain = 1. Master AND/OR rules and you can handle any placement question.

📐All Formulas
Core Formula
P(E) = Favourable / Total
Count outcomes where event happens ÷ all possible outcomes.
🔑 P always between 0 and 1. P(impossible)=0, P(certain)=1
Complementary Rule
P(not E) = 1 − P(E)
Probability of event NOT happening = 1 minus probability of happening.
💡 Use when "at least one" or "NOT" appears in question
AND Rule — Independent Events
P(A∩B) = P(A) × P(B)
Both events happen. Events are independent (one doesn't affect other).
🔑 "Both red" → P(red) × P(red) = multiply
OR Rule
P(A∪B) = P(A)+P(B)−P(A∩B)
Either A or B happens. Subtract intersection to avoid double counting.
🔑 "Either... or..." → OR rule
Mutually Exclusive
P(A∪B) = P(A) + P(B)
Events that can't happen together (coin: head OR tail). P(A∩B) = 0.
🔑 Head and Tail can't both occur simultaneously
At Least One
P(at least 1) = 1 − P(none)
Find probability of NONE happening, subtract from 1. Much easier!
💡 "At least one" → always use 1−P(none)

⚡Tricks & Shortcuts
🔥 "At Least One" Shortcut
Question: Probability of getting at least one Head in 3 coin tosses?
SLOW way: P(1H) + P(2H) + P(3H) = 3/8 + 3/8 + 1/8 = 7/8
FAST way: 1 − P(no heads) = 1 − (1/2)³ = 1 − 1/8 = 7/8
Always use the complement for "at least one" questions!

❌ Common Mistakes
WRONG: P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) always ❌ (only for mutually exclusive!)
RIGHT: P(A∪B) = P(A) + P(B) − P(A∩B) ✅

WRONG: Probability can be more than 1 ❌
RIGHT: Probability is always between 0 and 1 ✅

🧠Interactive Q&A
QUESTION 1 — EASY
A die is thrown once. What is the probability of getting an even number?
✅ C) 1/2

Even numbers on die: {2, 4, 6} = 3 outcomes
Total outcomes = 6
P(even) = 3/6 = 1/2
QUESTION 2 — MODERATE
A bag has 3 red and 5 blue balls. Two balls drawn at random. P(both red)?
✅ C) 3/28

P(both red) = 3C2 / 8C2
= 3 / 28 = 3/28
QUESTION 3 — EXAM MODERATE
Two coins are tossed. What is the probability of getting at least one tail?
✅ C) 3/4

Method: 1 − P(no tails) = 1 − P(both heads)
P(both heads) = (1/2)×(1/2) = 1/4
P(at least one tail) = 1 − 1/4 = 3/4

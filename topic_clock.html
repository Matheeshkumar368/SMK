🕐PART A — Clock Formulas

Memorize these 5 values first — everything else follows:
1 Hour = 30° | 1 Minute = 6° | In 12 hours: Hands meet 11 times | Right angle (90°): 22 times | 360° per 12 hours

Angle Between Hands
|30H − 11M/2|
H = hour, M = minutes. If result > 180, subtract from 360.
🔑 3:30 → |30(3) − 11(30)/2| = |90−165| = 75°
Coinciding Hands
Every 65 5/11 minutes
Hour and minute hands meet exactly every 65 5/11 minutes. In 12 hours: 11 times.
🔑 "How many times do hands coincide in 24 hours?" → 22 times
Opposite Hands (180°)
11 times in 12 hours
Hands are exactly opposite (180° apart) 11 times in every 12 hours.
🔑 Same count as coinciding! Both = 11 times in 12 hrs
Right Angle (90°)
22 times in 12 hours
Hands form 90° angle 22 times in 12 hours (twice as often as meeting).
⭐ This is Hexaware's favourite clock question!

📅PART B — Calendar Formulas
Leap Year Rule
Year ÷ 4 = Leap (mostly)
Divisible by 4 → Leap Year. BUT century years must be divisible by 400.
⚠️ 2100 NOT leap year (÷4 yes, but century not ÷400). 2000 IS leap year.
Odd Days
Normal year = 1 odd day
Leap year = 2 odd days
Odd days determine what day of week it will be in next year.
🔑 If Jan 1 2023 = Sunday, Jan 1 2024 = Monday (+1 odd day)
Day Calculation
Codes: Sun=0, Mon=1...Sat=6
Add odd days to current day code and take mod 7 to find future day.
🔑 Jan 1 is Sunday + 1 odd day → Monday
🔥 Odd Days in Months — Quick Reference
Jan=3 | Feb=0(non-leap),1(leap) | Mar=3 | Apr=2 | May=3 | Jun=2
Jul=3 | Aug=3 | Sep=2 | Oct=3 | Nov=2 | Dec=3

Trick to remember months with 31 days: Knuckle method (raised = 31, gap = 30/28)
⚡Shortcuts
🔥 Angle Shortcut — Verify Without Full Formula
At exactly 3:00 → Hour hand at 90° (3 × 30 = 90°)
At exactly 6:00 → 180°
At exactly 12:00 → 0° (both hands at 12)
But remember: Minute hand ALSO moves — use formula for non-exact times!
❌ Common Mistakes
WRONG: At 3:30, angle = 90° (just hour hand position) ❌
RIGHT: |30(3) − 11(30)/2| = |90 − 165| = 75° ✅ (minute hand moved too!)

WRONG: 2100 is a leap year ❌
RIGHT: 2100 ÷ 400 ≠ whole number → NOT a leap year ✅

🧠Interactive Q&A
QUESTION 1 — EASY
How many times do the hands of a clock form a right angle in 24 hours?
✅ B) 44

In 12 hours: 22 right angles
In 24 hours: 22 × 2 = 44 times
QUESTION 2 — MODERATE
What is the angle between the hour and minute hands at 4:20?
✅ A) 10°

Formula: |30H − 11M/2|
= |30(4) − 11(20)/2|
= |120 − 110| = 10°
QUESTION 3 — EXAM MODERATE
Which of the following is NOT a leap year?
✅ B) 2100

2000 ÷ 400 = 5 ✅ → Leap Year
2100 ÷ 400 = 5.25 ❌ → NOT Leap Year
2024 ÷ 4 = 506 ✅ → Leap Year (not a century year)
1600 ÷ 400 = 4 ✅ → Leap Year

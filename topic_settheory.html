SET THEORY & VENN DIAGRAMS

The formula n(A∪B) = n(A) + n(B) − n(A∩B) is the heart of this topic. Draw the Venn diagram, fill in the values from outside-in, and you'll solve any question in under 30 seconds.

📐All Formulas
Two Sets Union Formula
n(A∪B) = n(A)+n(B)−n(A∩B)
Total in either A or B = A alone + B alone + Both - double counted intersection.
🔑 Most tested formula. Find "total" or "at least one" questions.
Only A (not in B)
Only A = n(A) − n(A∩B)
People in A but not in B = Total A minus those in both.
🔑 "Only cricket players" type questions
Only B (not in A)
Only B = n(B) − n(A∩B)
People in B but not in A.
🔑 Always draw Venn diagram first!
Three Sets Formula
n(A∪B∪C) = n(A)+n(B)+n(C)−n(A∩B)−n(B∩C)−n(A∩C)+n(A∩B∩C)
For three overlapping sets. Subtract pairs, add back triple intersection.
🔑 Used in surveys: "likes all three", "likes exactly two" etc.
Neither A nor B
Neither = Total − n(A∪B)
People in neither group = overall total minus those in either group.
🔑 "How many like neither?" questions
⚡Placement Strategy
🔥 Outside-In Method — Always Draw First!
Step 1: Draw two overlapping circles (A and B)
Step 2: Fill in the INTERSECTION first (middle): given "both" value
Step 3: Fill "Only A" = n(A) − Both
Step 4: Fill "Only B" = n(B) − Both
Step 5: Total = Only A + Only B + Both
This approach works for ALL Venn diagram questions!
❌ Common Mistakes
WRONG: n(A∪B) = n(A) + n(B) always ❌ (only if mutually exclusive!)
RIGHT: n(A∪B) = n(A) + n(B) − n(A∩B) ✅

WRONG: "Exactly two subjects" and "At least two subjects" are the same ❌
RIGHT: "Exactly two" excludes those in all three. "At least two" includes all three. ✅
🧠Interactive Q&A
QUESTION 1 — EASY
In a class, 30 students like cricket, 25 like football, and 10 like both. How many like at least one sport?
✅ B) 45

n(C∪F) = n(C) + n(F) − n(C∩F)
= 30 + 25 − 10 = 45 students
QUESTION 2 — MODERATE
In a survey of 100 people, 70 like tea, 50 like coffee, and 30 like both. How many like neither?
✅ C) 10

n(T∪C) = 70 + 50 − 30 = 90
Neither = Total − n(T∪C) = 100 − 90 = 10 people
QUESTION 3 — EXAM MODERATE
In a class of 50, 25 like Math, 30 like Science, and 10 like both. How many like ONLY Math?
✅ A) 15

Only Math = n(Math) − n(Both) = 25 − 10 = 15 students

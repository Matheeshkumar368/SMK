📐All Formulas
Formula 1 — Basic Ratio
a : b = a/b
Ratio of a to b. Always simplify to lowest terms. 6:9 = 2:3.
🔑 Trigger: "in the ratio", "divided in ratio"
Formula 2 — Finding Values from Ratio
Value = (ratio part / sum of parts) × Total
If ratio is a:b and total is T, then first part = a/(a+b) × T
🔑 Example: 3:5 total 400 → First = 3/8×400 = 150
Formula 3 — Proportion
a/b = c/d → a×d = b×c
Cross multiplication rule. If 4 items cost ₹20, find cost of 7 items.
🔑 Trigger: "if...then find", "proportional to"
Formula 4 — Compounded Ratio
(a:b) × (c:d) = ac:bd
Multiply corresponding terms of two ratios.
🔑 Example: (2:3) and (4:5) = 8:15
Formula 5 — Duplicate Ratio
a² : b²
Square of each term. Duplicate of 2:3 = 4:9
🔑 Trigger: "square", "area comparison"
Formula 6 — Sub-Duplicate Ratio
√a : √b
Square root of each term. Sub-duplicate of 4:9 = 2:3
🔑 Trigger: "square root", "side comparison"
Formula 7 — Inverse Ratio
b : a (swap)
Inverse of a:b = b:a. Used in inverse proportion problems.
🔑 Trigger: "inversely proportional", "more workers less time"
Formula 8 — Mean Proportion
b = √(a×c)
b is the mean proportion of a and c when a:b = b:c
🔑 Trigger: "mean proportional between"

⚡Shortcuts & Tricks
🔥 Quick Ratio Split Trick
Given ratio a:b and total T → First part = a/(a+b) × T
Example: Share ₹560 in ratio 3:4 → 3/7 × 560 = 240 | 4/7 × 560 = 320
Verify: 240 + 320 = 560 ✅
🔥 Cross Multiplication — Fastest Method
If a:b = c:d then a×d = b×c
Missing term: If 3:5 = x:20 → 3×20 = 5×x → x = 12
❌ Common Mistakes
WRONG: Ratio 2:3 means first = 2, second = 3 ❌
RIGHT: Ratio 2:3 means first is 2 PARTS, second is 3 PARTS of the total.

WRONG: 2:3 + 3:4 = 5:7 ❌
RIGHT: Ratios cannot be simply added. Find a common second term first.

🧠Interactive Q&A
QUESTION 1 — EASY
Divide ₹720 between A and B in the ratio 3:5. How much does A get?
✅ B) ₹270

A's share = 3/(3+5) × 720 = 3/8 × 720 = ₹270
B's share = 5/8 × 720 = ₹450 | Verify: 270+450 = 720 ✅
QUESTION 2 — MODERATE
The ratio of boys to girls is 3:4. If there are 84 students total, how many boys are there?
✅ B) 36

Boys = 3/(3+4) × 84 = 3/7 × 84 = 36
Girls = 4/7 × 84 = 48 | Verify: 36+48 = 84 ✅
QUESTION 3 — EXAM MODERATE
If A:B = 2:3 and B:C = 4:5, find A:B:C.
✅ C) 8:12:15

A:B = 2:3, B:C = 4:5. Make B common → LCM(3,4) = 12
A:B = 8:12, B:C = 12:15
Therefore A:B:C = 8:12:15

📐All Formulas — Complete Arsenal
Formula 1 — Percentage Increase
((New − Old) / Old) × 100
Use when: salary increased, population increased, marks increased, profit increased.
🔑 Trigger: "increased", "grew by", "rose by"
Formula 2 — Percentage Decrease
((Old − New) / Old) × 100
Use when: salary decreased, population fell, marks reduced, loss occurred.
🔑 Trigger: "decreased", "reduced by", "fell by"
Formula 3 — Find Actual Value
(Percentage × Total) / 100
Use when: "find 25% of 800", "30% of salary", "what is X% of Y".
🔑 Shortcut: 25% of 800 = 800/4 = 200
Formula 4 — Successive Percentage
a + b + (a×b)/100
Use for two consecutive percentage changes. If decrease, use negative values.
🔑 Example: +20% then +30% = 20+30+6 = 56% NOT 50%
Formula 5 — Reverse Percentage
Original = Value × (100 / (100 ± %change))
Use when: "after 20% increase price is ₹600, find original price".
🔑 Trigger: "original price", "before increase"
Formula 6 — Percentage of Percentage
a% of b% of x = (a×b×x)/10000
For compound percentage questions combining multiple rates.
🔑 Rarely tested alone, but embedded in harder questions.

💡 Build all % from 10% in 2 seconds
X is Y% more than Z
X = Z × (1 + Y/100)
When price/salary is said to be X% more than another value.
🔑 "Salary is 20% more than 5000" → 5000 × 1.2 = 6000
X is Y% less than Z
X = Z × (1 − Y/100)
When price/value is reduced by a percentage.
🔑 "After 30% discount on ₹1000" → 1000 × 0.7 = 700

⚠️ Why Not Simply Add? The Hidden +6%
When you apply +20% then +30%, the second 30% is applied on the ALREADY INCREASED value (₹120, not ₹100).
So: 30% of ₹120 = ₹36, not ₹30. That extra ₹6 = 6% is the ab/100 term in the formula.
⚡Shortcut Tricks & Mental Math
🔥 The 10% Method — Solve Any % in 2 Seconds
Find 35% of 800:
Step 1: 10% of 800 = 80
Step 2: 30% = 3 × 80 = 240
Step 3: 5% = half of 80 = 40
Step 4: 35% = 240 + 40 = 280 ✅ Done in 3 seconds!
🔥 Fraction Shortcuts — Never Multiply!
25% of 800 → Don't multiply! → 800 ÷ 4 = 200
50% of 960 → 960 ÷ 2 = 480
12.5% of 400 → 400 ÷ 8 = 50
33.33% of 900 → 900 ÷ 3 = 300
❌ Common Trap — Never Do This!
WRONG: 25% of 500 = 25 × 500 = 12500 ❌
RIGHT: 25 × 500 ÷ 100 = 125 ✅

WRONG: +20% then +30% = 50% total increase ❌
RIGHT: Use a+b+ab/100 = 56% ✅

WRONG: "10 years ago" → ADD to age ❌
RIGHT: AGO → SUBTRACT ✅

🧠Interactive Q&A — Test Yourself!
QUESTION 1 — EASY
The salary of an employee is increased by 20% and then increased by 30%. What is the total percentage increase?
✅ Answer: B) 56%

Method — Successive % Formula:
a = 20%, b = 30%
Total = a + b + (a×b/100)
= 20 + 30 + (20×30/100)
= 50 + 600/100
= 50 + 6 = 56%

Why not 50%? The second 30% is applied on ₹120 (already increased), not original ₹100. That extra 6% comes from 30% on the +20 rupees.
QUESTION 2 — MODERATE
A student scored 360 marks out of 500. What percentage of marks did the student score?
✅ Answer: C) 72%

Method — Basic Percentage Formula:
% = (Marks obtained / Total marks) × 100
= (360 / 500) × 100
= 0.72 × 100 = 72%

Shortcut: 360/500 → Simplify: 36/50 = 72/100 = 72% ✅
QUESTION 3 — EXAM MODERATE
A number is increased by 20% and then decreased by 20%. What is the net percentage change?
✅ Answer: B) -4%

This is Hexaware's #1 Trap Question!
Using Successive % formula with a = +20, b = −20:
Net = a + b + (ab/100)
= 20 + (−20) + (20 × −20)/100
= 0 + (−400/100)
= 0 − 4 = −4%

Intuition: 100 → +20% → 120 → −20% → 120 × 0.8 = 96. Net change = −4%.

🧩Memory Tricks & Mnemonics
🎯 Story Method — "INCREASE ADDS, DECREASE TAKES"
Imagine a shopkeeper:
When his sales INCREASE → (New−Old)/Old×100 → New goes on TOP because it's bigger
When his sales DECREASE → (Old−New)/Old×100 → Old goes on TOP because it was bigger
Rule: The BIGGER NUMBER always goes on TOP (numerator)
🎯 SUCCESSIVE: Remember "HIDDEN BONUS"
When you get two raises, there's a HIDDEN BONUS from the first raise being larger:
a + b + ab/100 (the hidden bonus)
+20% and +30% → bonus = 20×30/100 = 6% extra = Total 56%

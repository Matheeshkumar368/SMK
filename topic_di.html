📐DI Formula Toolkit
Percentage Change
((New−Old)/Old) × 100
Most common DI question. Find % increase/decrease between years or categories.
🔑 Always check: increase or decrease first
Ratio Comparison
A : B = A/B
Compare two values from the chart. Simplify the ratio.
🔑 "Ratio of sales in 2021 to 2022"
Percentage of Total
(Part/Total) × 100
For pie charts — if 36° out of 360°, it represents 10%.
🔑 1% = 3.6° in a pie chart
Average from Data
Sum of values / Count
Find average across years, categories, or groups.
🔑 "Average production over 5 years"
Absolute Difference
|Value1 − Value2|
Difference between two data points. Always positive.
🔑 "How much more/less?" questions
Pie Chart Degree to Value
Value = (Degree/360) × Total
Convert pie chart degree to actual value using total.
🔑 90° slice of ₹1000 total = 90/360 × 1000 = ₹250

⚡DI Strategy — The 5-Step Approach
1
Read the title — understand what data represents (units: thousands? lakhs?)
2
Scan options first — see if you can eliminate two obvious wrong answers
3
Read the question keyword — Ratio? % change? Average? Highest? Pick formula accordingly
4
Estimate before calculating — approximation often gets you close enough to eliminate 2 options
5
Calculate minimum — only compute what's necessary. Don't calculate all options.
🔥 Estimation Trick — Save 30 Seconds Per Question
If options are: 48%, 52%, 61%, 75%
And your approximate calculation gives ~50% → immediately pick 52%
Use approximation to eliminate, not to compute exact answer.
In Hexaware DI, elimination is faster than calculation!
❌ DI Traps to Avoid
TRAP 1: Reading wrong year from bar chart (off by one column) ❌
TRAP 2: Forgetting units (thousands vs lakhs) ❌
TRAP 3: Using Total for % calculation instead of Base year ❌
ALWAYS: Double-check your base value before calculating %
🧠DI Practice — Based on the Chart Above
QUESTION 1 — EASY
Using the bar chart: What was the percentage increase in sales from 2020 to 2021?
✅ B) 100%

2020 = 150, 2021 = 300
% increase = (300−150)/150 × 100 = 150/150 × 100 = 100%
QUESTION 2 — MODERATE
What is the average sales over all 5 years (2019-2023)?
✅ C) 250

Total = 200 + 150 + 300 + 250 + 350 = 1250
Average = 1250/5 = 250 lakhs
QUESTION 3 — EXAM MODERATE
Ratio of 2022 sales to 2019 sales?
✅ B) 5:4

2022 = 250, 2019 = 200
Ratio = 250:200 = 5:4 (divide both by 50)
